College Student Social Media Checklist: 5 Do's and 5 Don'ts 

The Do's:

Do curate a professional presence on platforms like LinkedIn to connect with peers and recruiters.

Do assume everything you post is public and permanent, even on private accounts or stories.

Do use social media to showcase your academic projects, coding challenges, or extracurricular achievements.

Do engage respectfully with others' content and participate in constructive industry or campus discussions.

Do regularly review your privacy settings and audit old posts that may no longer represent who you are.

The Don'ts:

Don't post negative comments or complain about professors, your college, or future employers.

Don't share sensitive personal information, such as your exact daily location, phone number, or ID cards.

Don't engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or heated arguments in public comment sections.

Don't post or share unverified news or questionable content that could damage your digital footprint.

Don't use unprofessional profile pictures or bios on platforms where recruiters are likely to look.
