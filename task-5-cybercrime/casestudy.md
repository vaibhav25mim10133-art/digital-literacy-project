Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud in Digital Purchases

UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a cybercrime where scammers deceive individuals into transferring money or revealing their UPI PIN, leading to unauthorized bank deductions.

In a typical realistic scenario, a college student looking to purchase a discounted activation key for a popular PC game online comes across a seller on a social media marketplace. The seller offers the game at a heavily reduced price and asks for payment via UPI.

Step-by-step, the scam unfolds as follows: First, the scammer builds trust by showing fake screenshots of successful transactions. Second, the scammer sends a "payment request" link on a UPI app instead of a "send money" number, falsely claiming the student needs to enter their UPI PIN to receive a cashback or complete age verification. Third, the student, eager to play, hastily enters the PIN. Instantly, funds are deducted from their account, and the seller blocks them.

College students are frequent targets because they actively seek budget-friendly deals online and frequently use fast payment apps. The consequences are immediate financial loss, compromised bank details, and significant emotional distress. Because UPI transactions are instant and directly linked to bank accounts, recovering the stolen funds is often difficult, leaving the student without the money or the promised product.
